Predicting Urban Development in Dakota and Scott Counties

Every year, more and more people move to urban areas, and more and more land is developed for urban use. In Minnesota, more than 80% of residents live in urban areas, and the seven-county metropolitan area makes up about 63% of the state’s total population. In this project, I created a fuzzy suitability model of Dakota and Scott counties to determine areas most suitable for urban expansion. This model was developed using information about urban expansion from 2000 to 2020 and was informed by the following explanatory variables: Distance to major roads, distance to secondary roads, drive time to urban centers, distance to water, land cover, and slope.

Dakota and Scott Counties Suitability for Urban Development

For this project, I used the Suitability Modeler in ArcGIS to create a fuzzy suitability surface for suitability of urban development. I found, downloaded, and organized the data for this project. Six explanatory variables were considered as outlined above, and thus, six feature classes had to be derived and prepared for the Suitability Modeler. This map displays the suitability of the rural areas of the two counties with green regions being the least suitable and red areas being the most.

Suitable Areas for Urban Development

Between 2000 and 2020, approximately 8% of land was developed (using the same definitions and data from the model). This map was created with this guideline, displaying potential regions for development over the next 20 year period.

Distribution of Suitability Scores

The distribution of suitability scores is right-skewed and appears bimodal with possibly a third peak. The majority of the land is not suitable for development, hence why the 92nd was chosen as an indicator of suitability. The multiple peaks are likely explained by the categorical variables in the model. The mean of the suitability scores is about 0.42, and the median is about 0.38—this further indicates that the distribution is right-skewed. The vast majority of the scores are between 0.32 and 0.61. There does not appear to be any outliers.
