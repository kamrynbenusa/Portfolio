Locating an Optimal Location for a Fire Station in St. Cloud

On Election Day 2024, St. Cloud voters approved a referendum that proposed a tax increase to fund the construction of a new fire station. In this project, I was asked to evaluate the current performance of fire stations and identify the ideal location for a new fire station, given the potential locations. The response time of a fire station is impacted by three components: call processing time (how long it takes 911 to understand the incident and notify the correct emergency service provider), turnout time (how long it takes a responder to depart the station after receiving the notifcation, and travel time (how long it takes for the responder to travel from the station to the scene of the incident). Travel time is the variable of interest in this project.

Current 4-Minute Service Area

The first part of this project was to evaluate the current performance of St. Cloud fire stations. There are currently four fire stations within the St. Cloud city boundary. The National Fire Protection Association sets a national travel time standard of 4:00 minutes. Thus, it was of interest to delineate a 4:00 minute service area for the four fire stations. To do this, I created a network dataset and then used this to create a 4:00 minute service area. In this analysis, I also calculated the length of the roads within this 4 minute service boundary to determine the proportion of roads in St. Cloud that are within 4 minutes of a fire station: 73.6%.

Potential 4-Minute Service Area

For this project, we were given two candidate sites for the new fire station. The goal was to maximize the coverage of streets within St. Cloud. To determine the optimal locaiton, I created a location-allocation analysis layer in ArcGIS. This layer was created to maximize coverage of roads when travelling away from the fire stations. The site that maximized coverage was located on County Road 136. Including this station, the 4:00 minute service area includes 80.3% of St. Cloud roads, a substantial increase from the proportion of serviceable roads without the new station.

Current vs. Proposed Route to Tech High School

A closest facility network analysis was conducted to determine the current closest fire station to Tech High School. The closest fire station had an approximately 7.6 minute travel time, nearly double the travel time standard. A second closest facility network analysis was conducted, including the proposed location for a new fire station. If the station were built, it would become the closest fire station to Tech High School, with a travel time of approximately 4.4 minutes. This is a substantial improvement from the current travel time and this emphasizes the effectiveness of this location for the neeew fire station.
