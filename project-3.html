Interpolating a Mass Balance Surface at Storglaciären

The mass balance of a glacier describes the total sum of all accumulation and ablation over a single hydrological year. When the annual net balance is positive, accumulation is greater than ablation, so the glacier will become larger. When the annual net balance is negative, ablation is greater than accumulation, so the glacier will become smaller. Using ablation stake data and snow depth soundings, I was tasked with interpolating multiple surfaces to estimate the annual net mass balance of Storglaciären.

Storglaciären Summer Ablation

Before creating these interpolated surfaces, it was critical to check all statistical assumptions. There was clear evidence of spatial autocorrelation for both the ablatoin stake data and winter snow depth data. Both sets of data were normally distributed. The interpolated surface was created using Empirical Bayesian Kriging. The mean summer ablation for Storglaciären was 2.4 MWE and the RMSE was 0.35.

Storglaciären Winter Accumulation

This interpolated surface was created using Empirical Bayesian Kriging. The mean winter accumulation for Storglaciären was 3.0 MWE and the RMSE was 0.64.

Storglaciären Net Mass Balance

This surface was created by subtracting the summer ablation data from the winter accumulation data. For the majority of the glacier, ablation was greater than accumulation. However, the areas where accumulation was higher than ablation, accumulation was much higher, hence why the glacier became larger with a mean net mass balance of 0.6 MWE.
